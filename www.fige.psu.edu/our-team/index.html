About Our Team

Jim Sauls

Program Director

Jim joined Penn State in May 2016. He has a background in Anthropology and History with a geographic focus on Latin America. Jim completed his Bachelors of Arts in Anthropology in 2010 at Illinois State University and then volunteered with the AmeriCorps for one year aiding refugees in Phoenix, Arizona. Afterwards, he moved to Spain where he worked in secondary public education. In 2013 he returned to the United States for graduate studies at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign and received his Masters of Arts in Latin American Studies in 2015. Most recently, he lived in Porto Alegre, Brazil conducting ethnographic research before relocating to State College.

Ana C. Caliz

Ph.D Student Coordinator
Ana is currently a Ph.D student in the Mathematics Department here at Penn State. She was born in Morelia, Michoacan, a city in Mexico and is bilingual in both Spanish (native) and English. She decided to join FiGE to meet people like her that decided to go out of their confort zone.

Being part of FiGE has been one of the more enriching experiences I’ve ever had, because I met a lot of awesome people and made good friends from all around the world.

When I came to Penn State last year (2016) it was the first time I was going abroad, so I didn’t have a lot of international experience. But having the opportunity to learn from my new friends about their culture has giving me a more complete and understanding view of the world. Moreover, being able of also share a little bit about my country has been very satisfying.
